Aura Admin Panel — Review Notes
v2 — integrated after live walkthrough
Prepared for the development team. Pragmatic, synthetic, action-oriented. Tags: [MUST] blocking before go-live · [SHOULD] strongly recommended · [NICE] beneficial later · [ON-SCREEN] confirmed live on staging · [NEW] not in v1 · [BUG] defect to fix.
Executive summary
The UI is moving in the right direction and the navigation is coherent. Before scaling the panel we need to (a) close four blocking items that affect security and brand credibility, (b) consolidate the client data model around a clear hierarchy with dynamic form behaviour, (c) align account types, naming conventions and role/permissions logic, and (d) declare global standards on audit, soft-delete and notifications. v2 integrates the on-screen findings and adds a prioritised action list.

4.3 Geographical model — separation of concerns
[NEW] "Regional Hub" dropdown is mixed. Today it lists regions (Southern Europe), country clusters (DACH, Benelux), continents (Africa, Americas), operational hubs (EMEA, APAC, LATAM), and commercial categories (Emerging Markets, Global Travel Retail). Split into two distinct fields:

• Region (geographical sub-scope, e.g. Southern Europe, MENA, ANZ)
[BUG] Country field placeholder. Today the Country textbox shows placeholder "e.g. Southern Europe" — a region, not a country. Replace with a controlled country list (ISO 3166-1) and a sensible placeholder ("e.g. Italy").

Separate fields. Company Name, Legal Entity Name, Regional Hub, Region, Country, Address.
Address Type selector. Headquarters / Legal Office / Local Office / Billing / Operational Office. Multiple addresses per entity should be supported.

9. Cross-cutting requirements
[NEW] Not in v1. These apply globally and should be declared as standards, not implemented per-section.
9.1 Data architecture
Audit fields on every entity: created_by, created_at, updated_by, updated_at.
Soft delete vs hard delete: "Removed" status implies soft delete — declare the policy explicitly. Hard delete restricted to Super Admin and gated behind confirmation.
Naming convention: "Add [Singular Entity]" across all CTAs.
Status taxonomy (applied consistently across all entities):
Status
Meaning / when it applies
Active
Operational record, fully visible and usable.
Pending
Submitted for approval, not yet approved/rejected.
Invited
Account created/invited but not yet activated by the user.
Suspended
Temporarily disabled. Reversible.
Removed
Soft-deleted. Hidden by default, recoverable via "Show Removed".
Rejected
Approval explicitly denied. Visible in Rejected tab.
9.2 Relationships
Agency ↔ Client. An agency typically serves multiple clients across geographies. Define and document the association model: junction table, scoping by country, start/end of engagement, status.
User ↔ Entity. Each user (Agency Admin, Client User, etc.) must be linked to a parent entity; declare cardinality (1 or many?).
9.3 Search, filter and bulk operations
Search and advanced filters on every list view (geography, status, hierarchy level, type).
Bulk import (CSV / Excel) and bulk export — at minimum for Client Companies and Agencies.
9.4 Notifications
Pending Approvals: notify assigned reviewer on new submission, escalate if stale.
Email / in-app notification preferences manageable per user.
9.5 Security and access
MFA, session timeout, password policy for internal accounts.
No demo credentials on production-facing environments.
Audit log for security-sensitive events (login, role changes, hard deletes, disclaimer publish).
9.6 Compliance
Data residency. Document where data is stored per legal entity. Material once Aura hosts multiple legal entities across geographies.
Retention policy. Define retention for soft-deleted records and audit logs.

12. Final view
The platform is credible at UI level. The risk now is committing to a data model that mixes regions, hubs and country clusters, and to account-type conventions that are still wrong on screen. Closing items 1–12 of the priority list before any client demo is the right next milestone. Sections 13–25 should follow immediately after, since they cement governance and protect scale. Items 26–30 are useful enhancements once the foundation is stable.
